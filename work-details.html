The Art Of Observing

In our fast-paced world, the simple act of observing often gets overlooked. This video, created at NYU Abu Dhabi, delves into the art of observing, particularly through people-watching. Our intention is to explore why we are drawn to observing our surroundings, be it nature, people, or everyday life. Observing is more than a passive activity; it is a powerful tool for learning and connecting with the world around us. By closely watching others, we can gain insights into human behavior, culture, and social interactions. It allows us to understand and empathize with diverse perspectives, fostering a sense of community and shared humanity. Similarly, observing nature helps us appreciate the intricate beauty and complexity of the natural world. It encourages mindfulness, reduces stress, and inspires creativity. By highlighting these aspects, our video aims to remind viewers of the importance of taking a moment to observe, to be present, and to truly see the world in all its richness and diversity. "The Art of Observing" seeks to inspire a deeper appreciation for the subtle details that often go unnoticed. It invites viewers to slow down, to look closer, and to find joy and meaning in the everyday act of observing.

Following the campus cats

Collaboration with Hamdah AlSuwaidi, Shereena AlNuaimi, Abbad Shazly, Lincoln Ahiadu.

"Following a Campus Cat" is an interactive web-based narrative experience designed to engage users with a simple yet compelling storyline involving choices that lead to different outcomes. The core concept revolves around a user entering a virtual campus and encountering two distinct cats, each with unique behaviours and consequences associated with interacting with them.

The Flow Of Life

collaboration with Hamdah AlSuwaidi, Muhammad Ahmed, Aayat Azim, Aadil Chasmawala.

“The Flow of Life” is an interactive project crafted to visually and sonically depict the human life cycle through various manifestations of water. Presented on a scrolling website, the project initiates with a solitary droplet of water and progresses through distinct phases: Birth, Childhood, Adolescence, Adulthood, Old age, and Renewal. Each stage is enriched with meticulously chosen sound effects and interactive elements, elevating the viewer’s journey through life into an immersive experience.
